Common Kingfisher

The Common Kingfisher catches fish by diving up to 10 inches into still water.

  • Other Names: Eurasian Kingfisher, River Kingfisher
  • Habitat: Eurasia and North Africa
  • Diet: Fish and aquatic insects
  • Weight: 1.2-1.6 oz.

This colorful bird is about the size of a sparrow. Special adaptations allow it to spot prey underwater. They nest in burrows next to rivers, and will lay 2-10 glossy white eggs at a time. In areas where rivers will freeze over, Kingfishers migrate south for the colder months.
